Disasters by Year and State

The visual shown below is a interactive visual that can take in a year(s) filter input, selected state(s) filter input or a selected natual disaster filter input. The visuals will change depending on the filters applied, altering the colors or size of the graphs. If no filter is applied to the graphs, they are going to show all years, states and natural disasters that have taken place. If you wish to select more than one item in either the graph or map be sure to CTRL + LClick each item and for different years just select each one on the top right.

These graphs are intended to show where most natural disasters have occured in each state, as well as the disasters caused in certain states and the disasters that occur within certain years.

It may be pretty easy that to notice on the graphs that California and Texas are really saturated with red. This is due to the amount of wild fires that have had occured for over the years in these two states. These environments are prone to being dry so it has some sense of being this way. This is further supported if we select the 'Fire', with no other filters active, column in the graph to show that places in the west are common for these natural disasters.

If we wish to look at some of the more recent (past 5) years, we can see that California has the most natural disasters in the US. Most of the natural disasters that have occured in the US appears to be Fires with 307 incidents and most of which are shown to occur in California.

Lastly, we can select a state such as South Carolina, with no other filters active, to show that hurricanes occur the most in this state with a number of 16 incidents. If we continue to select a few more states in the East Coast such as Georgia, North Carolina, and Virginia, the natural disasters begin to shift in the graph showing severe storms becoming the highest occured with a count of 49 and 42 incidents of hurricanes being second highest.

The point of these graphs are to show specific trends between areas and their climates such fires being more common in the West Coast, hurricanes in eastern US, and the Mid-West often experiencing severe storms. Playing around with the values shall show certain trends with natural disasters in the United States.

Disasters by Year and State

This heat map shows the average unique incident per year of each state dating back to 1953. This map is gives you more of a summarized view of each states history. With each year, some states seem to have more disasters occur than the others despite some of them getting more disasters on average. Using this map, it can be easier to determine which state is safer from natural disasters on average. If we take a look at the map, we can in fact see that certain states like California and Texas still have a higher average of disasters that occur but something that be unexpected is the fact that Oklahoma has the overall highest average which is not completely apparent in the first map above. This shows that these states average disasters more often and potentially contain more dangerous climates.
